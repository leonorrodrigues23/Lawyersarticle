How to Form an LLC Online: Step-by-Step Guide for 2026

Everything you need to know about forming an LLC online, from choosing a state to getting your EIN and operating agreement.

Sarah Thompson
Business Law Analyst
Published Mar 1, 2026

Forming an LLC is the single most important legal step for any new business owner. It separates your personal assets from business liabilities, provides tax flexibility, and gives your venture professional credibility. The good news? In 2026, you can form an LLC entirely online in as little as 10 minutes for as low as $0 plus your state's filing fee.

This guide walks you through every step of the process, from choosing your state to getting your EIN and operating agreement. If you want to jump straight to comparing the best LLC formation services, check our expert rankings.

Step 1: Choose Your State of Formation

Most businesses should form their LLC in the state where they physically operate. While Delaware and Wyoming are popular for their business-friendly laws, forming in a different state than where you operate means you'll need to register as a "foreign LLC" in your home state anyway—doubling your fees and paperwork.

When to Consider Another State

  • Delaware: Best for businesses planning to raise venture capital or go public. Delaware's Court of Chancery specializes in business disputes.
  • Wyoming: No state income tax, strong asset protection, and low annual fees. Good for online businesses with no physical location requirement.
  • Nevada: No state corporate income tax and strong privacy protections. However, fees are higher than many states.

Step 2: Choose Your LLC Name

Your LLC name must be unique within your state and typically must include "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company." Before settling on a name, verify availability through your state's Secretary of State website. Most online legal services include a name availability check in their formation packages.

Step 3: File Articles of Organization

The Articles of Organization (called "Certificate of Formation" in some states) is the document that officially creates your LLC. It includes basic information like your LLC name, registered agent, and principal office address. State filing fees range from $50 (Kentucky) to $500 (Massachusetts).

Online legal services handle this filing for you. Services like LegalZoom and Rocket Lawyer submit the paperwork electronically and track the status until approval.

Step 4: Appoint a Registered Agent

Every LLC needs a registered agent—a person or company authorized to receive legal documents on behalf of your business. You can serve as your own registered agent, but using a professional service ($100-$300/year) ensures you never miss important legal notices and keeps your personal address off public records.

Step 5: Create an Operating Agreement

While not required in every state, an operating agreement is essential for any LLC. It defines ownership percentages, profit distribution, management structure, and what happens if a member leaves. Without one, your state's default LLC laws apply—which may not align with your intentions.

Online legal services offer customizable operating agreement templates that cover all the essential provisions for $35-$150, versus $500-$1,500 for an attorney-drafted version.

Step 6: Get Your EIN

An Employer Identification Number (EIN) is essentially a Social Security number for your business. You need one to open a business bank account, hire employees, and file taxes. The IRS provides EINs for free through their website, and most online legal services include EIN obtainment in their formation packages.

Step 7: Open a Business Bank Account

Separating personal and business finances is critical to maintaining your LLC's liability protection. You'll need your Articles of Organization, EIN, and operating agreement to open a business checking account. This step cannot be skipped—commingling funds is the most common way business owners accidentally "pierce the corporate veil."

How Much Does It All Cost?

Here's a realistic cost breakdown for forming an LLC online:

  • State filing fee: $50-$500 (varies by state)
  • Online service fee: $0-$300 (depending on plan)
  • Registered agent: $0-$300/year (often included in formation packages)
  • Operating agreement: $0-$150 (included with some services)
  • EIN: Free (from IRS or included with service)
  • Total: $50-$1,250 depending on state and service level
