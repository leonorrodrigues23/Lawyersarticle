LegalZoom vs Rocket Lawyer: Complete 2026 Comparison Guide

A head-to-head comparison of the two biggest online legal platforms. Find out which is better for your specific legal needs and budget.

Rachel Adams
Legal Services Editor
Published Mar 3, 2026

LegalZoom and Rocket Lawyer are the two biggest names in online legal services, but they serve different needs with fundamentally different business models. LegalZoom operates primarily on a per-service basis, while Rocket Lawyer uses a subscription model. Choosing between them can save you hundreds—or cost you hundreds if you pick the wrong one for your situation. In this guide, we break down every difference that matters.

If you want to see how these two stack up against the full competitive landscape, check our comprehensive ranking of the best online legal services.

Business Model: Pay-Per-Service vs. Subscription

The most fundamental difference between LegalZoom and Rocket Lawyer is how you pay. LegalZoom charges per service—you pay for what you need when you need it. Rocket Lawyer charges a flat monthly subscription ($39.99/month) that includes unlimited document creation, attorney consultations, and document review.

This means LegalZoom is generally cheaper for one-off needs, while Rocket Lawyer provides better value if you have ongoing legal requirements. A small business owner who needs multiple contracts, an operating agreement, and occasional attorney advice will save significantly with Rocket Lawyer's subscription versus paying LegalZoom's per-document fees.

LegalZoom Pricing Overview

  • LLC Formation: $0 + state fee (Economy), $249 + state fee (Standard), $299 + state fee (Express Gold)
  • Last Will: $89 (basic), $199 (comprehensive)
  • Trademark: $199 + USPTO fees
  • Registered Agent: $299/year
  • Attorney Consultation: $0 for 30-min with paid plans

Rocket Lawyer Pricing Overview

  • Monthly Membership: $39.99/month
  • LLC Formation: Free with membership + state fee
  • Documents: Unlimited creation included
  • Attorney Consultations: Included in membership
  • Non-member pricing: $39.99-$99.99 per document

Service Range Comparison

LegalZoom offers a broader range of services overall. Beyond document creation and business formation, they provide a full suite of intellectual property services, compliance management, and even tax advisory through partnerships. Rocket Lawyer focuses more deeply on document creation and attorney access, making their core offerings more robust in those specific areas.

For business formation specifically, both platforms handle LLCs, corporations, and nonprofits effectively. LegalZoom has the edge in add-on services like annual report filing, while Rocket Lawyer excels in providing ongoing legal support through their subscription model.

Attorney Access and Quality

Rocket Lawyer includes attorney consultations as part of their membership, making it easy to get quick legal guidance without additional cost. LegalZoom offers attorney consultations through their legal plans, which start at additional monthly fees on top of per-service pricing.

Both platforms vet their attorney networks, but the experience differs. Rocket Lawyer's on-call attorneys are available for quick questions and document review, while LegalZoom's attorney network tends to handle more complex, involved legal matters. For routine questions, Rocket Lawyer is more convenient. For complex legal needs, LegalZoom's deeper attorney relationships may be more valuable.

User Experience

LegalZoom has invested heavily in their user experience, with a modern interface, clear pricing pages, and a straightforward checkout process. However, the upsells during checkout can be aggressive—you'll be offered multiple add-ons that can significantly increase your total cost if you're not careful.

Rocket Lawyer's interface is clean and functional, with their guided document creation being particularly well-designed. The Q&A format walks you through each section, making it easy even for people with no legal knowledge. Their membership dashboard makes it simple to manage all your documents and access attorney support.

When to Choose LegalZoom

  • You need a one-time service (LLC formation, trademark filing)
  • You want access to the broadest range of legal services
  • You prefer to pay per-service rather than commit to a subscription
  • You need complex legal services beyond document creation
  • Brand reputation and track record matter to you (22+ years, 4M+ customers)

When to Choose Rocket Lawyer

  • You need ongoing legal support (multiple documents, regular consultations)
  • You want unlimited document creation at a flat monthly rate
  • Attorney consultations are important to you
  • You're a small business owner with regular contract needs
  • You want to try before committing (7-day free trial available)

The Verdict

Neither platform is universally "better"—the right choice depends entirely on your usage pattern. For occasional, one-off legal needs, LegalZoom's per-service model is more cost-effective. For ongoing legal support with regular document creation and attorney access, Rocket Lawyer's subscription provides significantly better value.

Many savvy users actually use both: LegalZoom for major one-time filings (like initial LLC formation with their $0 + state fee deal) and Rocket Lawyer for ongoing document needs and attorney consultations. Compare all top legal services to find the best fit for your specific situation.
