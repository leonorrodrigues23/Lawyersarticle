Do I Need a Lawyer? When DIY Legal Services Are (and Aren't) Enough

A practical guide to knowing when you can handle legal matters yourself and when you should hire a professional attorney.

David Martinez
Senior Legal Writer
Published Feb 27, 2026

The rise of online legal services has made it possible to handle many legal tasks yourself at a fraction of the traditional cost. But knowing when DIY is appropriate—and when it's a costly gamble—is crucial. Making the wrong call can cost you far more than the attorney fees you were trying to save.

This guide provides a practical framework for deciding when to use online legal services versus hiring a traditional attorney.

When DIY Legal Services Are Sufficient

Online legal platforms excel at standardized, routine legal tasks. If your situation fits neatly into a common template and doesn't involve disputes or complex negotiations, DIY is often the smart choice.

Business Formation

Forming a basic LLC or corporation is a straightforward filing process. Online services handle this efficiently for $0-$300 plus state fees, versus $1,000-$2,500 with an attorney. The documents are standard, the process is well-defined, and the risk of error is low.

Simple Estate Planning

Basic wills, powers of attorney, and healthcare directives for people with straightforward wishes (spouse inherits everything, children are guardians, etc.) are well-served by online platforms. The templates are attorney-drafted and state-specific.

Standard Contracts

NDAs, freelance agreements, basic lease agreements, and standard business contracts are routine documents that online platforms handle well. The key is that both parties are entering into a standard arrangement without unusual terms.

When You Absolutely Need a Traditional Attorney

Some situations carry too much risk or complexity for template-based solutions. Here's when to invest in professional representation:

Any Type of Litigation

If someone is suing you or you need to sue someone, you need an attorney. Period. Court proceedings have strict procedural requirements, and a single misstep can cost you the case regardless of the merits.

Criminal Matters

Criminal charges, DUI defense, expungement applications—these all require specialized legal expertise. The stakes (potential jail time, criminal record) are too high for self-representation.

Complex Estate Planning

If your estate involves business interests, properties in multiple states, blended family dynamics, special needs beneficiaries, or assets exceeding the estate tax threshold ($13.61M in 2026), you need an estate planning attorney.

Employment Disputes

Wrongful termination, discrimination claims, or severance negotiations involve specialized employment law and often significant monetary stakes. An employment attorney typically works on contingency for employee-side claims.

The Hybrid Approach: Best of Both Worlds

Many savvy consumers use a combination strategy: online services for document creation and routine filings, with an attorney review for anything high-stakes. For example, you might create your LLC operating agreement online for $100, then pay an attorney $200-$300 for a one-hour review to ensure it covers your specific situation.

This approach gives you the cost savings of online services with the quality assurance of professional oversight. Compare the top online legal platforms to find the right starting point for your legal needs.
